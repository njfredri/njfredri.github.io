About

Nathaniel Fredricks is a Doctoral student at the University of Arkansas. He earned his bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering in May of 2023. Nathaniel started working in the Computer Systems and Design Lab (CSDL) under Dr. David Andrews in 2021. During his time there, he learned how to use multiple tools such as Vivado and Xilinx SDK. For his honors thesis, he created a virtual machine for the SPAR-2 array processor. This virtual machine would interpret instructions and translate them to fit the appropriate specifications of any SPAR-2 configuration. As SPAR-2 had to scale in order to fit onto different FPGAs with different resources, the virtual machine would allow the same portable code to work across all these different configurations.

After recieving the Distinguished Doctoral Fellowship at University of Arkansas, Nathaniel decided to continue working under Dr. David Andrews for graduate school. His current interests are in FPGA architectures and Processing-In-Memory (PIM). Nathaniel is researching ways to create FPGA architectures that are better suited for PIM designs. He is currently using OpenFPGA and COFFE for this research. Future research plans include implementing non-CMOS technology, such as memristors, on FPGAs. With the University of Arkansas's new silicon fab being constructed, Nathaniel plans on having his designs fully fabricated.

Application Development Intern, J.B. Hunt
June, 2020 to August, 2020; June, 2021 to August, 2021

Nathaniel Fredricks completed 2 summer internships at J.B. Hunt. During his time there, he worked on both the front end and back end of web applications.

During his first internship, Nathaniel worked on a team to help develop a new interview tool for J.B. Hunt. During his second internship, Nathaniel helped to develop a batch process for debugging J.B. Hunt's shipping web application.
